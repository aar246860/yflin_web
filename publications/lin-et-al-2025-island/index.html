Image-Well Solution for Island Aquifers with Pumping, Recharge, and Complex Coastlines

Ying-Fan Lin, Barret L. Kurylyk, Adrian D. Werner, Chih-Yu Liu, Cristina Solorzano-Rivas, Jun-Hong Lin · Advances in Water Resources · 2025

DOI 10.1016/j.advwatres.2025.104977
Research question

Can analytical speed be retained when an island has wells, recharge, and a complex coastline?

The study asks how freshwater-lens head and interface depth can be evaluated without reducing every island to a simple symmetric boundary.

Physical system

A freshwater lens under multiple environmental and pumping stresses

An unconfined island aquifer with a freshwater-saltwater interface, spatial recharge, pumping or injection wells, and a coastline represented through image-well constructions.

Method

Analytical superposition coupled to image wells

A new analytical freshwater-lens solution is combined with image wells so repeated pumping and recharge scenarios can account for coastline geometry without a full numerical model for each trial.

Result

Head and interface depth become fast constraints for scenario testing

Convergence tests and comparisons with an established solution support the formulation, and an island example based loosely on Kinmen demonstrates multi-well pumping optimization subject to an interface-depth limit.

Video

Animated research summary

The animation explains the paper's physical and mathematical logic. Conceptual curves are not presented as new field observations.

Evidence boundary

The paper uses analytical and numerical checks plus an illustrative optimization example. No observational field data were used in the published study.

The solution is conditioned on its sharp-interface, aquifer-property, boundary, and superposition assumptions. The demonstration is not a site-specific safe-yield estimate for Kinmen.

Citation

Lin, Y.-F., et al. (2025). Advances in Water Resources, 201, 104977.
