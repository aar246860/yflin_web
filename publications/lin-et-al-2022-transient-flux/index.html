Analytical Solution for Estimating Transient Vertical Groundwater Flux from Temperature-Depth Profiles

Ying-Fan Lin, Chia-Hao Chang, Jui-Pin Tsai · Journal of Hydrology · 2022

DOI 10.1016/j.jhydrol.2022.127920
Research question

What is missed when vertical groundwater flux is forced to remain constant?

The paper examines whether repeated temperature-depth profiles can recover a changing vertical groundwater flux rather than one time-invariant value.

Physical system

Heat transport through a vertically flowing subsurface

A one-dimensional subsurface heat-transfer problem driven by time-varying surface temperature and vertical groundwater flow, observed through temperature-depth profiles.

Method

Transient analytical heat transfer used inside an inverse problem

The analytical solution predicts temperature through depth and time, allowing candidate flux histories to be evaluated efficiently against observed profiles.

Result

The inverse target becomes a flux history

The analytical solution agrees closely with a finite-element reference, the numerical experiment recovers the prescribed transient flux, and the field estimate is reported to be close to measured flux.

Video

Animated research summary

The animation explains the paper's physical and mathematical logic. Conceptual curves are not presented as new field observations.

Evidence boundary

Support comes from numerical verification, a synthetic inversion experiment, sensitivity analysis, and a field application. The website animation is conceptual rather than a digitization of the field record.

Flux estimates remain conditioned on the heat-transfer model, thermal properties, boundary temperature history, profile sampling, and inversion setup.

Citation

Lin, Y.-F., Chang, C.-H., & Tsai, J.-P. (2022). Journal of Hydrology, 610, 127920.
