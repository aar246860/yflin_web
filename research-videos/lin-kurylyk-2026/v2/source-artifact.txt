RESULT AND INTERPRETATION BOUNDARIES

- The two field examples are the Tuolumne River transect and the Meghna River
  transect described in the article.
- The field parameters are effective, frequency-band- and scale-dependent
  descriptions of periodic head behavior. They are not asserted to be unique
  microscopic material constants.
- The classical and lagging models are compared with the same retained
  observations, while AIC explicitly penalizes added model parameters.
- Multiple separated frequencies and at least two distances are needed for the
  full parameter set. When only one frequency is available, one lag parameter
  is fixed before estimating the remaining parameters.
- Analytical and numerical agreement checks the implementation of the selected
  attenuating branch; it does not by itself prove that every field mechanism is
  uniquely identified.
