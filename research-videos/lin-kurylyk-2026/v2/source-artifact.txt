                                              ABSTRACT
Keywords:
Lagging model                                 Periodic head fluctuations in aquifers are often analyzed with a diffusion model that links amplitude damping
Phase-amplitude mismatch                      and phase lag to a single hydraulic diffusivity, yet diffusivities inferred separately from phase and from ampli
Periodic groundwater signals                  tude often disagree. This mismatch arises in heterogeneous, dual-porosity, leaky, or partially unsaturated settings
Coastal aquifers                              where fast and slow pathways decouple the amplitude response from the phase response. Herein, Darcy's law
Tidal head analysis                           is empirically modified with two field-scale time constants within a causal memory model with a form that can
                                              be reduced to a dual-domain model but can accommodate more complex heterogeneity. An included flux lag
                                              denotes the time required for discharge pathways to readjust after a change in gradient, including redistribution
                                              along high-conductivity paths, short-term storage or release, and near-boundary resistance. The head lag denotes
                                              the time required for a head difference to develop across communicating domains under finite exchange, as in
                                              mobile-immobile or fracture-matrix systems, leaky units, or shallow unsaturated zones. The solution predicts that
                                              flux lag dominates long-period waves, head lag controls short-period waves, and their interaction governs signal
                                              transfer for intermediate periods. Parameters are estimated from multi-frequency observations at two contrast
                                              ing aquifers. In an unconfined coarse-gravel aquifer (Tuolumne Meadows, USA), the model yields a 2.28-day
                                              flux lag and a 0.12-day head lag, reduces root-mean-square error by a factor of four, and yields a diffusivity
                                              that aligns with prior diffusivity values. Along a semi-confined sandy alluvial transect beside the Meghna River
