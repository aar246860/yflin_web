# Analytical Modeling of Grout Heat Storage Effects in Thermal Response Tests

## Research question
When does grout heat storage significantly affect thermal response interpretation, and how should it be represented to separate formation-scale thermal conductivity from well-level heat storage effects?

## Core mechanism
- Early-time thermal curvature is often dominated by borehole grout heat capacity.
- The model introduces mechanism separation so that formation parameters are not over-fitted to early-time signals.
- The approach supports faster and more stable parameter estimation from TRT data.

## Scene sketch
1. Pumping-test-like thermal excitation of a geothermal borehole is initiated.
2. Near-well temperature signal rises with a delayed component from grout heat storage.
3. Late-time trend is interpreted as formation response once grout transients relax.
4. Interpretation check compares model partitions against standard conduction-only assumptions.
5. Design-relevant implications are expressed as parameter-uncertainty reduction in system-level thermal design.

## Validation logic
- Use synthetic and available field-scale response checks to evaluate identifiability between grout heat capacity and formation thermal conductivity.
- Quantify fit improvement primarily in early time without forcing a biased late-time conductivity estimate.

## Conclusion
The paper’s contribution is methodological separation, not a blanket statement that all TRT biases are due to grout.
