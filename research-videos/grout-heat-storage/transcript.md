# Analytical Modeling of Grout Heat Storage Effects in Thermal Response Tests

## Abstract
How does grout heat storage alter early-time temperature recovery and thermal parameter estimates in thermal response tests?

## Key script
- The study models thermal response tests in shallow geothermal settings where the borehole grout can store heat.
- Instead of treating thermal response as coming only from formation conduction, the analysis includes grout heat storage as an explicit model mechanism.
- The model tracks multiple time scales that separate early-time grout buffering from later-time formation conductivity and volumetric-heat-parameter information.
- The framework aims to accelerate and stabilize inversion while reducing parameter confounding at short test duration.
- A practical outcome is reduced bias in inferred field-scale thermal parameters used for design decisions.

## Conclusion
Including grout heat storage improves interpretation fidelity by shifting some early-time thermal response from formation parameters into a mechanism-specific term.
