# A Pumping Test with Hydraulic Memory

## Abstract
Can a pumping-test model represent a delayed relation between radial water flux and drawdown gradient while retaining finite-well effects in a leaky confined aquifer?

## Key script
- We start from a finite-radius pumping well in a leaky confined aquifer.
- The classical immediate-porous-media flux-gradient relation is extended to include lag terms for flux and drawdown-gradient response.
- The two lags can be different, so the response can become non-instantaneous in the early-time range.
- When the two lags are equal, their effect is not identifiable in the constitutive relation and only contributes as synchronized delay.
- Setting both lags to zero recovers the classical Darcy relation.
- The study compares this delayed relation with a seven-day Rapid City constant-rate pumping test around RC-5.
- Five observation wells are used to evaluate fitted lag pairs and to show the spread among wells.
- The model improves early-time agreement in that field case, while lag estimates are interpreted as test-model diagnostics and remain test-conditioned.
- This prevents direct interpretation of lag as one universal material constant.

## Conclusion
The delayed constitutive framework provides a bounded, physically interpretable alternative to purely empirical early-time correction terms in selected pumping-test settings.
