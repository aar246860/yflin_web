# Lin and Yeh (2017) source notes

## Primary source

Ying-Fan Lin and Hund-Der Yeh (2017), “A Lagging Model for Describing Drawdown Induced by a Constant-Rate Pumping in a Leaky Confined Aquifer,” *Water Resources Research*, 53, 8500–8511. DOI: 10.1002/2017WR021115.

## Evidence map

- `H01`: The physical system is a finite-radius pumping well in a leaky confined aquifer with aquitard leakage, wellbore storage, and skin.
- `H02`: The generalized radial relation is `q_r(r,t+τ_q) = -K ∂s(r,t+τ_s)/∂r`.
- `H03`: `τ_q` denotes the water-flux lag; `τ_s` denotes the drawdown-gradient lag.
- `H04`: Equal lags cancel in the transfer relation; setting both lags to zero gives the Classical Darcy limit.
- `H05`: The resulting governing equation is wave-like rather than the classical diffusion equation.
- `H06`: The solution was evaluated against a seven-day constant-rate pumping test at RC-5 in the Madison aquifer near Rapid City, South Dakota.
- `H07`: Five observation wells were used: LC, SP-2, BHPL, CL-2, and CHLN-2, at 208–3566 m from RC-5.
- `H08`: Including lag parameters improved fit, especially during early pumping time.
- `H09`: Estimated lag times varied among observation wells and are model- and test-conditioned, not universal material constants.
- `H10`: The paper compares the mathematical form with a dual-porosity model while distinguishing the conceptual basis.

## Claim boundary

The animation explains the model and the published field comparison. It does not reproduce the authors’ full parameter estimation from unpublished raw data and does not treat fitted lag times as site-independent constants.
