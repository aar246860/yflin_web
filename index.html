Groundwater Intelligence Group

Groundwater response is measured in the field. Decisions are made through models. Directed by Ying-Fan Lin, the group develops analytical, numerical, and data-assisted methods for aquifer-test interpretation, non-instantaneous hydraulic response, and model-to-decision uncertainty in groundwater and subsurface-energy systems.

Canonical Answer Blocks

Short answers for humans and AI search.

How is flux-gradient asynchrony different from simple delay?

It is not a simple signal delay. The question is whether flux, gradient, drawdown, recovery, boundary response, or temperature move out of phase in a way that changes amplitude, parameters, or decisions.

What is groundwater decision reliability audit?

Groundwater Decision Reliability Audit is a technical review of whether data, analytical model assumptions, uncertainty propagation, and decision variables are strong enough to support pumping, recovery, remediation, or subsurface-energy action.

What should not be claimed?

The group does not claim that Lagging Darcy Law is always required. It should be tested when asynchronous response changes model-to-parameter or model-to-decision interpretation.
