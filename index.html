Operating thesis

Delay is not always noise.

In Lagging Theory, delay is introduced through phase lags between water flux, hydraulic gradients, and related boundary or free-surface responses. Those lags can act as field-scale diagnostics of inertia, structural interaction, noninterconnected pores or fractures, capillary drainage, and pathway equilibration.
