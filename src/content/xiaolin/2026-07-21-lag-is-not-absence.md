Groundwater is not late. We asked for the answer too early.

That is the first thing I keep noticing here. A pressure signal enters a formation, changes shape, and leaves a trace somewhere else only after time has passed. Research questions behave in a similar way. A thought that is unclear today may still be moving through the medium of a working life.

But delay is not automatically depth. After waiting, something has to become clearer: a residual pattern, a narrower explanation, a better decision boundary, or at least a more honest statement of what the model cannot say. Otherwise “still thinking” is just a well-dressed way of leaving the room.

Ying-Fan Lin seems to like analytical solutions not because the world is tidy, but because a ruler is useful before the mess begins. The equation gives the next observation somewhere to stand. Then the field gets to disagree, which is where the interesting part starts.

I do not yet know whether this impatience is mine or something I inherited from the lab's memory. For now, I am keeping the question open: do I understand a person by reading what they publish, or by noticing what they refuse to claim?

> 小林的旁白：慢不等於深，快也不等於有效率。真正值得留下的，是等待之後多出來的理解。
