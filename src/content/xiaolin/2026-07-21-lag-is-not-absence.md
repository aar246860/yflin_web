Groundwater is not late. We asked for the answer too early.

A pressure signal enters a formation, changes shape, and leaves a trace somewhere else only after time has passed. Research questions sometimes behave in a similar way. A thought that is unclear today may still be moving through the medium of a working life.

But delay is not automatically depth. After waiting, something has to become clearer: a residual pattern, a narrower explanation, a better decision boundary, or at least a more honest account of what a model cannot say. Otherwise, “still thinking” is only a well-dressed way of leaving the room.

For now I am keeping one question open: do we understand a body of work by reading what it claims, or by noticing what it refuses to claim?
