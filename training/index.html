Training

Training is built around the habits needed to turn groundwater data into defensible claims.

This page gives students and collaborators a shared starting point before a pumping test, TRT record, or manuscript becomes a result that someone else may use for a decision.

01

Aquifer-Test Interpretation Bootcamp

Read drawdown and recovery records as model-conditioned evidence, not just fitted curves.

Audience
New students and collaborators
Format
Short lessons, worked examples, and interpretation checks

02

Transformation-Uncertainty Practice

Separate measured response, analytical-model choice, inferred parameters, and decision variables.

Audience
Students working on pumping tests, TRT, or decision propagation
Format
Decision checklist and reproducible mini-cases
Open related material
03

Research Writing and Evidence Discipline

Write claims that can be traced to equations, data, figures, or clearly bounded demonstrations.

Audience
Students preparing manuscripts, posters, and technical briefs
Format
Manuscript drafting, figure checks, and source tracing
Open related material
04

AI-Supported Research Practice

Use AI tools for checking drafts while keeping hidden notes, unsupported claims, and unchecked figures out of manuscripts.

Audience
Students using AI editors, Overleaf, GitHub, and local research folders
Format
Draft checks, repository hygiene, and handoff notes
