About the research program

Ying-Fan Lin studies delayed hydraulic response in groundwater and subsurface decision problems.

His work uses analytical models to define physical reference behavior, numerical experiments to test interpretation pathways, and AI-assisted tools to examine residual structures that classical models leave unresolved. The goal is not a single best-fit parameter. The goal is a traceable interpretation framework that can explain flux-gradient delay, audit uncertainty, and support engineering decisions.

2016-2020

Analytical well hydraulics and boundary physics

Exact and semi-analytical models establish the reference behavior for pumping, Robin-type boundaries, solute diffusion, heat transport, and near-well effects.

2017-2026

Lagging Theory and groundwater memory

Lagging Theory starts from phase lags between water flux and hydraulic gradient, then extends to free-surface drainage, periodic head signals, subsidence, and field time series.

2024-2026

Transformation uncertainty

The current TU_Lag line separates measured response from interpreted parameters, auditing how model pathways propagate into design decisions.

2026+

Subsurface energy intelligence

TRT, aquifer thermal response, shallow geothermal systems, and industrial water-energy planning become the application surface for groundwater-aware delay diagnostics.
